Same Pace Plan Tool

For busy parents • practical in 10-15 minutes

The real-life problem

One of you wants change now. The other needs more time.

That gap can feel scary when you are tired and stretched thin. It does not mean your relationship is broken.

Do this tonight (10-15 minutes)

  1. Set a 10-minute timer and sit side by side (not across like a debate).
  2. Person A says: "In one year, I hope family life feels like ___." Keep it to one minute.
  3. Person B does the same. No fixing, no pushback.
  4. Each person says: "The pace I can handle this week is ___."
  5. Pick one small step for tomorrow that both of you can do (example: 15-minute walk after bedtime, no phones).

Copy/paste script

"I love us. I don't want to push you, and I don't want us stuck. Can we do a quick 10-minute plan and pick one next step?"

Kid-chaos version (when you're exhausted)

  • Do 5 minutes during cleanup and 5 minutes after bedtime.
  • Too wiped out to talk? Send one text each: "My hope is ___." and "My pace this week is ___."
  • If voices rise, pause for 20 minutes. Come back and finish one step only.

What “better” looks like in 7 days

  • You had at least one calm talk about the future
  • You both followed through on one small step
  • Less “all or nothing” pressure

Keep it simple: Repeat this once a week until the pace feels fair to both of you.
