Relationship Fit Questions

For busy parents • practical in 10-15 minutes

The real-life problem

You’re stuck in your head, wondering, “Are we just tired, or are we truly off track?”

With kids, chores, and work, every week can feel like survival mode. Confusion is normal.

Do this tonight (10-15 minutes)

  1. Set a 10-minute timer.
  2. Take turns answering 3 quick questions:
    • Do I feel safe and respected with you right now?
    • Do we still want the same kind of home life?
    • Am I willing to put in effort this month?
  3. No arguing. Just listen and say, “Thanks for being honest.”
  4. Pick one next step for tomorrow (example: schedule counseling, do a 10-minute check-in, or pause big talks for 48 hours).

Copy/paste script

“I’m not trying to start a fight. I want us to get clear. Can we do a 10-minute check tonight and pick one next step?”

Kid-chaos version (when you’re exhausted)

  • Do it while driving, folding laundry, or right after bedtime.
  • Too many interruptions? Do one question now, two later.
  • If either of you gets flooded, pause for 20 minutes and come back.

What “better” looks like in 7 days

  • Less second-guessing about your relationship
  • One clear plan you both agreed to
  • Fewer late-night stress talks

Rule: If you both said “yes” to effort, test the plan for 2 weeks before changing it.
