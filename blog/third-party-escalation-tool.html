Get Backup Tool

For busy parents • practical in 10-15 minutes

The real-life problem

You keep having the same fight. Both of you walk away hurt and tired.

That is common when you are raising kids and running on empty. Getting help is a strength, not a failure.

Do this tonight (10-15 minutes)

  1. Set a 10-minute timer.
  2. Each person answers: “Do we want outside help with this?”
  3. If yes, pick one option now: therapist, coach, pastor, or trusted mentor.
  4. Before bed, one person sends two texts to ask for openings this week.
  5. The other person puts a follow-up reminder on tomorrow’s calendar.

Copy/paste text

“I love you. I don’t want us stuck in this same fight. Can we take 10 minutes tonight and ask for help?”

Kid-chaos version (when you’re exhausted)

  • Do it while cleaning up dinner, in the car, or after bedtime.
  • If kids interrupt, do 5 minutes now and 5 minutes later.
  • Too tired to book tonight? Send this one text: “Hi, we need support. Do you have any openings this week?”

Quick action checklist (easiest version)

  1. Pick one tiny step from this tool for tonight.
  2. Do it in under 10 minutes.
  3. Text or tell your partner one clear next step for tomorrow.
  4. Rate helpfulness 1-10 before bed and keep only what works.

What “better” looks like in 7 days

  • You sent at least one message for help
  • You have one next step on the calendar
  • Tough talks feel less lonely

Rule: Asking for help is a win. Keep moving, even if the first person says no.
