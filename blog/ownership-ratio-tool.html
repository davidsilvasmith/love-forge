Own Your Part Tool

For busy parents • practical in 10-15 minutes

The real-life problem

Every hard talk turns into "your fault" vs "no, your fault."

When you are juggling kids, work, and no sleep, this is common. It does not mean you are failing.

Do this tonight (10-15 minutes)

  1. Set a 10-minute timer.
  2. Person A says: “My part in this was…” and names one thing they did.
  3. Person B does the same. No interrupting. No defending.
  4. Each person picks one tiny repair step for tomorrow (example: "I will ask before I assume" or "I will take bedtime so you can reset").

Copy/paste script

“I’m not trying to win this. I want us to feel better tonight. Can we do a quick 10-minute reset and each own our part?”

Kid-chaos version (when you’re exhausted)

  • Split it up: 5 minutes now, 5 minutes after bedtime.
  • Can’t talk live? Trade two short voice notes.
  • If either of you gets too heated, take 20 minutes, then finish.

Quick action checklist (easiest version)

  1. Pick one tiny step from this tool for tonight.
  2. Do it in under 10 minutes.
  3. Text or tell your partner one clear next step for tomorrow.
  4. Rate helpfulness 1-10 before bed and keep only what works.

Tool: Own Your Part Tool

What “better” looks like in 7 days

  • Fights end sooner
  • Less blame, more teamwork
  • You feel calmer in the same hard moments

Keep it simple: If this helps even a little, keep using it this week.

How to use this tool effectively

  • When to use it: Use Own Your Part Tool when this exact problem is happening now, not as a general reading exercise.
  • How often: Run it once today, then repeat 3-7 times before judging results.
  • Common mistake: Changing multiple things at once. Use one tool at a time so you can see what works.
  • If it feels awkward: Use the script exactly once, keep the first run under 10 minutes, and rate helpfulness 1-10.
